How to Work with GPG Keys

GPG Keys with Thunderbird

Thunderbird makes it extremely simple to use GPG keys to send encrypted emails. Here is a simple step-by-step guide to get you started.

Create a GPG Key Pair

  1. Click on the hamburger button on the top right
  2. Click on "Account Settings"
  3. Click on "End-to-end Encryption" under the email address you want to generate a key for
  4. Click on "Add Key", then "Create a new OpenGPG Key" and continue
  5. Set the desired parameters, then click on "Generate key"

How to Share Your Public Key

  1. Start an email
  2. Click on the downwards caret next to "Security"
  3. Click on make sure that the "Attack my Public Key" box is ticked

Import A Public Key

  1. Click on the hamburger button on the top right
  2. Click on "Account Settings"
  3. Click on "End-to-end Encryption" under any email
  4. Click on "OpenPGP Key Manager"
  5. Click on "File", then "Import Public Key(s) from file"

Every Email After

  1. After you and someone else exchange public keys, select "Require Encryption" in every email. This will encrypt the email and sign it.
  2. Click on "Security" and select the recipient.
